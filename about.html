Backstory

I first taught myself HTML when I was in high school, making my Xanga page look cool, but I have a theory that it was my career as a baker that uniquely prepared me for a role in software engineering. I spent most of the last ten years reverse-engineering recipes and producing consistent results in fast-paced, high-pressure environments without ever sacrificing my attention to detail. I worked on teams that collaborated to deliver a final product in a time crunch, and I found unique solutions to unexpected problems when I was alone in the kitchen at 5 AM. I decided to pursue web development more seriously after watching many restaurants really struggle to get an effective online platform up in the early weeks of the pandemic.

What I'm Looking For

I am frontend/backend ambidextrous, but I'm most interested in niche UI and accessibility problems, and software that cares more about people than venture capital.
